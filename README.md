# Bright-TV

## Overview of the presentation:
- *Sections*: Introduction, About Us, Usage Trends, Viewership Trends, Marketing Strategy, Conclusion
- *Introduction*: Focuses on growing BrightTV's subscription base and strengthening customer engagement through user profile analysis and viewing 

## Key points from the slide:
- The study analyzed user demographics, viewing patterns, and session behavior.
- *Usage Trends* include graphs on:
    - Province by Age: males have higher usage in most provinces, especially Gauteng.
    - Gender by Age: comparing usage across ethnic groups for males and females.
    - Province by Social handle: breakdown of users by province.
    - Gender by UserID: males outnumber females.

## Insights:
- Males have higher usage in most provinces and in the black ethnic group.
- Data could be useful for targeting specific demographics in South Africa.

## Key points:
- *Viewership Trends*:
    - Bar chart compares viewership across channels ("SABC 1" and "E.TV" have higher viewership).
    - Pie chart shows channel viewership by month (Jan, Feb, Mar).
- *Marketing Strategy*:
    1. Target Gauteng and Western Cape.
    2. Focus on male audience.
    3. Create gender-specific promotions.
    4. Leverage social media.

## Key observations:
- *Gender by Age*: A bar graph shows distribution by gender and age.
- *Sum of Age by Province and Gender*: Tables break down data by province (like Gauteng, Western Cape) and gender (female, male).
- *Province by Age*: A line graph shows trends by province.

## Data highlights:
- Gauteng has a significant sum of age (57951 total, 26140 female, 31811 male).
- Western Cape has 27665 total (11517 female, 16148 male).

- *Conclusion*: Audience is predominantly male in Gauteng and Western Cape. Targeted content and promotions are recommended.
